Message from the Bishopric

This time of year is one of my favorites as it reminds me to be thankful and grateful for all that I have. During Thanksgiving, my grandfather asked me a question of what I should be thankful for. I listed many things. He then asked a follow up question: What are you thankful for? That second question made me ponder and ask myself, "Are there things in my life that I should be thankful for, that I am not?" As I pondered this question, I realized so many things that I take for granted that I should be more mindful of, and give thanks to my Heavenly Father for blessing me so abundantly.

I am grateful above all for a Savior who loves me, who gave His life to save all of mankind. I'm grateful for the spirit that testifies this truth to me, and for my Heavenly Father who blesses me when I keep the commandments and stay true to the covenants I have made.

Tonight is the 2025 First Presidency's Christmas Devotional
